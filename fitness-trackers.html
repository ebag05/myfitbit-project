Your Health - How to be a Fit Bit

In New York City, obesity is epidemic: more than half of adult New Yorkers are overweight (34%) or obese (22%). Data show that obesity begins early in life: nearly half of all elementary school children and Head Start children are not a healthy weight. In New York City, 1 in 5 kindergarten students, and 1 in 4 Head Start children, is obese. People (adults and children) who are overweight are at increased risk for diabetes heart disease, stroke, high blood pressure, arthritis, and cancer.

Tools to Help You Track Progress

As you're on the path of reclaiming your "Fit Bit", some people will gain strength and muscle while others will lose a tremendous amounts of weight and body fat (depending on your goals and the lifestyle changes you make); despite the differing goals and vastly different results, there's one specific change that will help you to truly get the results you want:

Track everything you do.

If you're serious about making changes to your life and getting in shape, I cannot stress enough the importance of tracking your progress, and I don’t mean just stepping on the scale every morning and freaking out every time it goes up 1/10th of a pound. I’m talking about tools that allow you to find and stay on the right path.
